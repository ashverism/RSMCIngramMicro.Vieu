The GTM Challenge

HubLife is differentiated and deploys inside the customer's own AWS account — which is exactly why it is a go-to-market challenge, not a product one. Because the data, agents, and platform live in the buyer's environment, every deal has to clear delivery credibility and responsible-AI, security, and governance review before it advances — pulling in mission, IT, finance, security, and compliance stakeholders at once. As a 2025-founded, 8-person team, RSMC cannot brute-force that with cold outbound. The constraint is speed to the right buying committee, through trusted access, in four distinct motions.

Motion 1 · Nonprofit & Public Sector

Motion 2 · Regulated — Health & Life Sciences
Consensus, not vision, is the blocker
For academic medical centers, teaching hospitals, and clinical research, a customer-hosted agentic AI deal requires HIPAA / GxP governance proof and a wide approval map. Reach: CIO, CISO / security, compliance, data owners, and the business sponsor.
Motion 3 · Channel via Ingram / Xvantage
Coordinate the ecosystem players
HubLife is marketed through Ingram Micro's Xvantage and AWS Marketplace Channel Partner Private Offers. Without coordinated intros, deals get stuck between ecosystem players even when fit is strong. Reach: distributor, reseller, alliance, and end-customer execs.
Motion 4 · SMB AI
Lean teams, fast confidence
SMB leaders need quick confidence that a customer-deployed AWS solution is practical, cost-manageable, and partner-backed before they engage. Reach: founders / operators who own IT, operations, and budget in small buying teams.
